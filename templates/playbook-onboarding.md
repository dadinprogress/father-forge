# Playbook: Client Onboarding

**Category:** Playbook  
**Use case:** First 30 days with new client  
**Timeline:** 30 days  
**Goal:** Successful implementation + high satisfaction

---

## Overview

This playbook ensures new clients succeed in the first 30 days, setting them up for long-term satisfaction and referrals.

---

## Week 1: Setup

**Day 1: Welcome Call**
- Confirm project scope and timeline
- Answer any questions
- Set expectations clearly
- Introduce team members

**Day 2: Information Gathering**
- Send detailed intake questionnaire
- Request access to systems
- Collect relevant documents

**Day 3: Kickoff Meeting**
- Present project plan
- Walk through timeline
- Assign point of contact
- Set weekly sync schedule

**Day 5: First Deliverable**
- Share preliminary findings or assessment
- Get feedback
- Adjust approach if needed

---

## Week 2-3: Execution

**Daily:**
- Advance project work
- Document progress
- Flag any blockers immediately

**Weekly Sync (same time, same day):**
- 30-minute check-in
- Review progress against plan
- Address blockers
- Confirm next week's work

**By Day 21:**
- Core deliverables should be 80% complete
- Client feedback should be incorporated
- No surprises allowed

---

## Week 4: Completion & Transition

**Day 25:**
- Final deliverable ready for review
- Client tests/validates
- Make requested revisions

**Day 28:**
- Final sign-off
- Provide documentation
- Schedule handoff call

**Day 30:**
- Completion call
- Gather testimonial/case study
- Discuss next phase (if applicable)
- Request referrals

---

## Communication Rules

- **Always over-communicate** (weekly updates minimum)
- **No surprises** (flag issues immediately)
- **Document everything** (decisions, changes, feedback)
- **Respond within 24 hours** (even if just "I'll have answer by Friday")
- **Be proactive** (suggest improvements, not just reaction)

---

## Potential Issues & Responses

**Client goes silent:**
- Send check-in email day 2 of silence
- Follow up with call day 3
- Never wait on the client; keep working

**Scope creep:**
- Document all requests
- Confirm if they're in/out of scope
- If out of scope, add to "Phase 2" list
- Don't deliver out-of-scope work without approval

**Quality concerns:**
- Address day 1, don't wait for final delivery
- "Is this meeting expectations?" questions
- Revise before moving forward

**Timeline pressure:**
- Be honest immediately
- Offer reduced scope or delayed delivery
- Never commit to impossible timelines

---

## Success Metrics

- **On-time delivery:** 100% (no exceptions)
- **Client satisfaction:** 9/10 or higher
- **Testimonial/referral:** 100% of completions
- **Next phase conversion:** 40%+

---

## After Day 30

- **Success:** Schedule retrospective, pitch next phase, ask for testimonial
- **Partial success:** Understand what fell short, fix it, earn the referral
- **Struggle:** Over-deliver on something, get them a win, build trust for next phase

## Key Insight

The first 30 days make or break the relationship. Clients remember how you treated them at the start.

Onboard like they're a Fortune 500 company, even if they're a 2-person startup.
